Liver Cancer

Liver cancer, also known as hepatic cancer, is a significant health concern worldwide, with high mortality rates and increasing incidence. Understanding the patterns, risk factors, and outcomes associated with liver cancer is crucial for developing effective treatment strategies and improving patient outcomes. This analysis utilizes a dataset of liver cancer patients to explore various aspects of the disease, including demographic characteristics, clinical features, and survival outcomes.

Lung Caner

Lung cancer remains one of the leading causes of cancer-related mortality worldwide. Despite advancements in detection and treatment, the prognosis for lung cancer patients often remains poor, particularly when diagnosed at advanced stages. This analysis aims to provide a comprehensive overview of lung cancer by examining patient data, identifying key patterns, and uncovering factors that impact patient outcomes.

Breast Cancer

Breast cancer is one of the most common cancers affecting women worldwide and is a leading cause of cancer-related mortality. Despite significant advancements in early detection, diagnosis, and treatment, the prognosis for breast cancer patients can vary widely based on various factors. This analysis seeks to provide a comprehensive overview of breast cancer by examining patient data, identifying key patterns, and uncovering factors that influence patient outcomes.

Country Cancer Data

Cancer is a global health issue with significant variations in incidence, prevalence, and mortality rates across different countries. Understanding these variations is crucial for developing effective public health strategies and improving cancer outcomes worldwide. This analysis aims to provide a comprehensive overview of cancer data across multiple countries, identifying key patterns, and uncovering factors that influence cancer incidence and outcomes.

Common Caner

Cancer remains a leading cause of morbidity and mortality worldwide, with certain types of cancer being more prevalent than others. This analysis aims to provide a comprehensive overview of the most common cancer types, including breast, lung, prostate, and colorectal cancers. By examining patient data, we seek to identify key patterns, risk factors, and outcomes associated with these cancers.
